01 · The Problem

Why financial literacy
from an early age?

Research findings:
Encouraging financial habits early significantly improves decision-making in adulthood. Adolescence is the key moment to transform impulsive spending into strategic planning.

In Colombia, teenagers aged 12 to 17 face multiple distractors that encourage impulsive spending. Digital banking tools help young people understand concepts like inflation, savings and returns, building more responsible financial skills from an early age.

Emilio became interested after noticing impulsive spending in his surroundings and after saving for his own iPhone. That discipline was key when a veterinary emergency arose for his pet.

📊

1 — The right moment to build habits

Surveys of teenagers aged 12–17 identified impulsive spending patterns, allowing the platform's educational approach to be adapted accordingly.

🎓

2 — Youth reality in Colombia

An investment of 500,000 pesos at 8.7% annual interest helped understand inflation and real money returns in real time.

💡

3 — A personal motivation with real impact

Programmed saving helped co-finance a 3-million-peso veterinary treatment, proving the practical value of consistent saving habits.

02 · Glossary

Key concepts

What nobody teaches us in school — but that changes everything.

🐜

Ant Expenses

Small daily expenses — snacks, apps, quick purchases — that seem harmless individually but quietly drain your budget. Controlling them builds financial discipline and protects your future goals.

📈

Compound Interest

The progressive growth of money saved and invested consistently, where the interest earned also generates new interest over time. Einstein called it "the eighth wonder of the world."

🗓️

Budget

The responsible organization and tracking of money received periodically. It means recording income, expenses, savings and progress toward personal goals — helping you make informed decisions.

🎯

Savings Goal

A specific financial objective — a phone, a trip, a console — set for the short, medium or long term. A clear goal helps control spending and evaluate every financial decision against what you truly want.

😤

Impulsive Spending

Buying driven by emotion, social pressure or the need for instant gratification, without thinking about future consequences. The "72-Hour Rule" — waiting before buying — helps distinguish a want from a need.

🏦

Saving vs. Investing

Saving means storing money for future goals or emergencies. Investing means using that money to generate returns. Young people can access digital options offered by banks and learn to protect their resources from inflation.

05 · The Project

About this research

🙏 Acknowledgements

Special thanks to my parents, Omar Ruiz and Amira Monroy, for their trust and support throughout this process. Their guidance gave this project its human and practical dimension.

🏫 Colegio San Viator

This project was developed as the IB MYP Personal Project at Colegio San Viator, Bogotá, Colombia, under the guidance of the faculty advisors.

🎯 My learning goal

My goal was to encourage saving in young people and transform impulsive spending into strategic planning. I aimed to develop solid financial management skills, inspired by my own year-long savings journey to buy my iPhone. My research focused on how to make informed financial decisions.

🔍 What I found researching

I discovered that apps and games drive impulsive spending in teenagers my age. The most impactful concept was compound interest and how money grows with consistency. I also analyzed how inflation affects our capital and verified real returns of 8.7% in digital investments.

💡 How my relationship with money changed

I now consciously control my leisure expenses and maintain a programmed savings habit for two years. I saved $180,000 pesos by buying clothes in January instead of December. Thanks to this, I was able to co-finance a 3-million-peso veterinary emergency, validating my personal effort.

📬 My recommendation for other teenagers

My key advice is to apply the "72-Hour Rule" to avoid impulsive purchases and distinguish needs from wants. Start saving with clear goals — this will give you the freedom to acquire what you truly value and the financial resilience to face any future challenge.

🤔 A reflection

I believe adolescence is a privileged stage because many needs are already covered by the family. That means money received as gifts or allowances can be used smarter — to save, invest and learn about finance. It's a valuable opportunity to build intelligent habits that can generate peace of mind and economic freedom in the future.
